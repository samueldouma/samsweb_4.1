Epidemic Ferment is a hybrid research-publication and experimental magazine emerging from my Senior Integrated Project at Kalamazoo College. It investigates the social, psychological, and neurological effects of life within algorithmic media systems—what might be called a civilization-scale attention experiment. Through a mix of essay, theory, and field documentation, the project examines how digital infrastructures shape perception, memory, and collective behavior, and how these forces have come to define contemporary consciousness.

Printed as a magazine and accompanied by an evolving digital archive, Epidemic Ferment functions as both a study and a provocation: a documentation of the brain-rot age and a gesture toward fermentation—toward synthesis, renewal, and mindful participation in the informational ecosystem. What follows is some associated material as well as the means to acquire a copy of it yourself.
